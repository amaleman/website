About

I'm a PhD candidate in Chemical Engineering at Stanford University, working in the Jaramillo Group on one of the defining challenges of the clean energy transition: why electrocatalysts degrade — and how to make them last.

My thesis focuses on cobalt-based materials during oxygen and hydrogen electrocatalysis. I combine cutting-edge in-situ techniques — EC-MS, ICP-MS, synchrotron-based XAS and GI-XRD — to watch catalysts change in real time under operating conditions.

Beyond the lab, I lead outreach programs, DEI initiatives, and mentorship efforts to open doors in STEM for underrepresented students.

Research

What I Work On

⚗️

Electrocatalyst Stability

Investigating degradation mechanisms of cobalt-based thin films during oxygen evolution (OER) and hydrogen evolution (HER) in both acidic and alkaline conditions. Establishing routes to improve Co stability as a function of operating conditions and strategic synthesis.

OERHERThin Films
🔬

Multimodal In Situ Characterization

Developing experimental platforms that simultaneously monitor electrocatalytic activity, material stability, and product generation. Combining EC-MS, ICP-MS, AFM, XRD, and synchrotron-based XAS and GI-XRD for real-time surface dynamics.

EC-MSICP-MSSynchrotron
⚡

Sustainable Energy Systems

Bridging fundamental electrochemistry with industrial-scale technologies — from PEM water electrolyzers and fuel cells to anion exchange membrane devices — to accelerate the clean hydrogen economy.

PEMAEMClean H₂
