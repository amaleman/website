About Me

Hi, I'm Ashton — electrocatalysis researcher at Stanford

I'm a PhD candidate in Chemical Engineering at Stanford University, advised by Professor Thomas F. Jaramillo. My dissertation builds a holistic understanding of how cobalt-based catalysts degrade during oxygen and hydrogen electrocatalysis — and what we can do about it.

I believe science should drive impact. My work sits at the intersection of fundamental materials science and real-world energy challenges: making green hydrogen production cheaper, more durable, and smarter by treating degradation as design information rather than a failure mode.

Outside research, I'm deeply committed to building equitable pathways into STEM through mentorship, outreach, and community leadership.
