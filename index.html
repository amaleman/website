Ashton Aleman

PhD Candidate in Chemical Engineering at Stanford University

I study how materials behave — and break — under the extreme conditions required for sustainable energy technologies. My work focuses on electrocatalysis, combining time-resolved experiments and advanced characterization to understand how catalysts evolve in real time.

Research

My research aims to develop a holistic understanding of degradation mechanisms in cobalt-based electrocatalysts for water splitting and fuel cell applications.

I design experimental platforms that simultaneously probe activity, stability, and product formation using techniques such as ICP-MS, EC-MS, and synchrotron X-ray methods.

Ultimately, I’m interested in bridging fundamental electrochemistry with real-world energy systems.
